Set-up:

1.
Cover surface of chemical flow hood with absorbent blue pads and a layer of paper towels
2.
Add the foam lid on top of the paper towels
3.
Add 4 g of sucrose to each of the scintillation vials
Will be used to make 20% sucrose/10% formalin
4.
Fill each of the 20mL scintillation vials with formalin
Shake until sucrose dissolves. Place on ice
5.
Add formalin to Erlenmeyer flask and place on ice
6.
Add filtered NaCl to one of the 100mL beakers
7.
Plug in the perfusion pump.
Attach the blunted needle
Using NaCl to test, adjust the flow so that there is a pulse of fluid ejection, like a heart beat
8.
Add ~1/3 of of a pipette of isoflurane onto the kimwipe within the conical tube

Additional notes

Perfusion:

Note: This is primarily for reference, and not intended to have to check each step as you are actually doing the perfusion
You'll be working with formalin, so if you are checking off on a device, you would need to use a bag to cover your phone and mark as formalin contaminated
Track the timing as you go in your physical paper notebook, and then transfer information for the perfusion timing widget
1.
Scruff the mouse and anesthetize with the isoflurane in the conical tube
2.
Once anesthetized, keeping the conical tube over the mouse's head, transfer to the foam lid
3.
Pin down each limb into the foam lid
4.
Open the mouse's body cavity to expose the diaphragm
5.
Cut a hole in the diaphragm, then cut along the lateral edges of the ribs
6.
Use the hemostat to grasp the base of the rib cage and then fold back towards head to expose the heart
7.
Insert the blunted needle into the left ventricle; use the apex of the heart as a guide
You want this needle to be stable. Determine for yourself if this is most consistently achieved by holding the needle yourself during the saline rinse, or by using other needles to stabilize the pump tubing
8.
Clip the right atrium
9.
Turn on the pump to perfuse filtered saline through the mouse for ~2 min until the liver has cleared of blood
10.
Turn off the pump. Switch the line to formalin. Turn back on the pump. Perfuse with formalin for 10 min
11.
Turn off the pump. Remove the needles from the heart and the limbs
12.
Extract the brain from the skull. Note the visual condition of the brain
13.
Place the brain in one of the 20mL scintillation vials filled with 20% sucrose / 10% formalin. Note the time. Place the vial back on ice.
14.
Rinse the pump lines with saline before starting next animal
15.
Carefully pour the formalin + blood waste from the lid into the waste container, using the funnel
16.
Place the animal in the black trash bag

Clean-Up:

1.
Place all items to be discarded that touched formalin into the labeled black trash bag (bodies, paper towels, blue pads, etc)
2.
Run clean water through the perfusion pump. Discard initial washes in formalin waste container
3.
Rinse off instruments and containers. Add tergazyme to each item and allow to soak for a few minutes
4.
Rinse off the tergazyme. Soak items in bleach
5.
Carefully rinse off bleach. Run 3x under tap water and 3x under milliQ
6.
Ensure formalin waste container is closed. Store in fume hood.
7.
Wipe down hood surface with ethanol

Brain Prepation:

1.
After 90 min, replace the 20% sucrose / 10% formalin in the scintillation vial with 20% sucrose
